3. The Timer: A Watchdog for Fairness
The OS sets up a timer to act like a watchdog. Here’s how it works:

The OS sets a counter (like a countdown clock) for each program.

This counter is decremented (counted down) by the physical clock of the computer (a hardware component that ticks at regular intervals).

When the counter reaches zero, the timer generates an interrupt.
